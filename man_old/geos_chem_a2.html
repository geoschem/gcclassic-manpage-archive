Appendix 2: GEOS–CHEM Horizontal Coordinates

The GEOS–CHEM model can be run at the following horizontal resolutions:

  • 4° latitude x 5° longitude (entire globe)
  • 2° latitude x 2.5° longitude (entire globe)
  • 1° latitude x 1° longitude (sub-region of globe)

We are currently precluded by computational limitations from running a global simulation at 1° x 1° resolution. Nevertheless, we have the capability to perform GEOS–CHEM simulations for selected sub-regions of the globe at 1 x 1 resolution. At present, we have implemented two such nested-grid regions:

Yuxuan Wang has defined a nested-grid over China and SE Asia which extends from

  • 70°E —150°E longitude
  • 11°S — 55°N latitude

Qinbin Li and Rynda Hudman have defined North American nested grid which extends from

  • 140°W — 40°W longitude
  • 10°N — 60°N latitude

These sub-regions cover approximately 10% of the entire globe, and is sufficient to allow us to perform detailed NOx-Ox-hydrocarbon chemistry simulations with our current computing hardware.

All 1° x 1° nested-grid simulations must use the GEOS–3 meteorology, because GEOS–1 and GEOS–STRAT meteorologies have a maximum horizontal resolution of 2° x 2.5°, and because the native resolution of GEOS–4 is not 1° x 1° but rather 1° x 1.25°.
